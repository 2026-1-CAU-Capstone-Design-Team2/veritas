## Summary
This work introduces the World–Value–Action (WAV) model, a unified framework that enables implicit planning in Vision–Language–Action (VLA) systems by learning a structured latent representation of future trajectories conditioned on visual observations and language instructions. Unlike direct action prediction, WAV learns a world model to predict future states and a trajectory value function to evaluate long-horizon utility, reformulating action generation as inference in this latent space where the model concentrates probability mass on high-value, dynamically feasible trajectories. Theoretical analysis demonstrates that direct planning suffers from an exponential decay of feasible trajectory probability with increasing horizon, whereas latent-space inference reshapes the search distribution toward feasible regions, enabling efficient long-horizon decision making. Extensive simulations and experiments show WAV consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods in task success rate, generalization ability, and robustness, particularly in long-horizon and compositional scenarios.

## Key Points
- Introduces the World–Value–Action (WAV) model as a unified framework for implicit planning in VLA systems.
- Learns a latent distribution over future trajectories conditioned on visual observations and language instructions.
- Uses a learned world model to predict future states and a trajectory value function to evaluate long-horizon utility.
- Reformulates action generation as inference in the latent space, concentrating probability mass on feasible high-value trajectories.
- Demonstrates that latent-space inference mitigates exponential decay of feasible trajectory probability inherent in action-space planning.
