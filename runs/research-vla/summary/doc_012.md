## Summary
LAP-3B introduces a general VLA pre-training recipe (Language-Action Pre-training) that unifies low-level actions with vision-language supervision, achieving substantial zero-shot generalization to novel robot embodiments. The method demonstrates superior performance over state-of-the-art open-source baselines by learning transferable control representations and enabling efficient fine-tuning on real-world robots.

## Key Points
- LAP-3B is the first VLA to demonstrate strong zero-shot transfer to unseen robot embodiments.
- The approach unifies low-level actions with vision-language supervision for robust generalization.
- LAP-3B achieves over 50% average success on previously unseen robot embodiments compared to state-of-the-art baselines.
- Fine-tuning is significantly more efficient than prior methods, requiring only a fraction of training steps and demonstrating superior data/compute efficiency.
- The method enhances language-following ability and enables seamless co-training with vision-language objectives for precise action generation.

## Reliability Notes
- The paper cites the PolaRiS template as its basis.
- Results are based on qualitative rollouts across multiple embodiments under zero-shot transfer.
- Data and computational efficiency improvements are explicitly reported in real-world manipulation benchmarks.
- The study includes a comprehensive analysis of fine-tuning efficiency compared to prior methods.
