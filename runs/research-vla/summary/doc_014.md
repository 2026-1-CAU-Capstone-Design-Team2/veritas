## Summary
This work presents the first demonstration of accurate and reliable open-loop optimal control of a soft continuum robot (SCR) using interpretable, video-learned latent dynamics without camera feedback. The authors introduce Visual Oscillator Networks (VONs) with attention-based decoding (ABCD) to achieve low MSE tracking errors on real systems. Key contributions include an interactive SCR live simulator for target design and ablation studies demonstrating that explicit latent dynamical models enable stable long-horizon control in scenarios where full-state feedback is limited.

## Key Points
- First demonstration of interpretable, video-learned latent dynamics enabling reliable open-loop control of SCRs without camera feedback.
- VONs with ABCD attention maps consistently reduce image-space tracking error compared to baselines.
- An interactive SCR live simulator supports design of unseen or extrapolated targets for open-loop control.
- Ablation studies confirm that specific architecture choices contribute to stable long-horizon performance.
