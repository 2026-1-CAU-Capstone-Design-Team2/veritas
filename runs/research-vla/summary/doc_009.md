## Summary
Being-H0.5 introduces a human-centric VLA framework that treats human interaction traces as a universal 'mother tongue' to bridge physical language gaps between diverse robotic platforms. The model utilizes UniHand-2.0, a large-scale corpus of over 35,000 hours spanning 30 distinct embodiments, to unify heterogeneous supervision and train a single foundation model capable of perceiving, describing, and acting across robots like Franka+Inspire and Unitree G1. Key innovations include the Unified Action Space, Mixture-of-Flow (MoF) architecture, Manifold-Preserving Gating, and Universal Async Chunking to ensure robust cross-embodiment generalization and real-world deployment.

## Key Points
- Proposes a human-centric learning paradigm treating human interaction as a universal 'mother tongue' for physical interaction.
- Introduces UniHand-2.0, the largest embodied pre-training recipe with over 35,000 hours of multimodal data across 30 robotic embodiments.
- Unifies heterogeneous supervision through a Unified Action Space to enable low-resource robots to bootstrap skills from human data.
- Utilizes Mixture-of-Flow (MoF) and Manifold-Preserving Gating to decouple shared motor primitives from embodiment-specific experts.
- Achieves state-of-the-art results on LIBERO (98.9%) and RoboCasa, demonstrating strong cross-embodiment capabilities across diverse platforms.
