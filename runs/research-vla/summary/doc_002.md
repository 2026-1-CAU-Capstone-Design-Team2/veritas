## Summary
The document presents seven distinct research papers addressing advancements in Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for robotics and autonomous systems. Key contributions include PixelVLA, which enhances pixel-level understanding and multimodal prompting; X-VLA, a scalable soft-prompted Transformer architecture; DEAS, an offline RL framework leveraging action sequences; AutoBio, a simulation benchmark for biology labs; NavFoM, a cross-embodiment foundation model; Unified Diffusion VLA, which couples generation and action via denoising; HybridVLA, which fuses autoregressive and diffusion prediction methods; FlowAD, an ego-scene interactive modeling framework; and Vlaser, a synergistic embodied reasoning model. These works collectively aim to improve robot control in complex environments by addressing challenges in pixel-level understanding, cross-embodiment heterogeneity, long-horizon planning, and multimodal interaction.

## Key Points
- PixelVLA improves manipulation success rates by $10.1\%\sim28.7\%$ over OpenVLA while requiring only $1.5\%$ of pretraining cost.
- X-VLA introduces a novel Soft-Prompt approach to leverage heterogeneous robotic data sources and cross-embodiment features.
- DEAS leverages temporally extended action sequences to reduce planning horizons in offline reinforcement learning.
- NavFoM achieves state-of-the-art performance across seven public benchmarks by integrating diverse camera configurations and temporal contexts.
- Unified Diffusion VLA optimizes the generation of actions and images jointly through a synchronous denoising diffusion process.
- HybridVLA innovatively combines autoregressive and diffusion prediction paradigms to enhance control robustness.
- FlowAD proposes an ego-scene interactive modeling paradigm that effectively considers ego-motion feedback for autonomous driving.
- Vlaser bridges embodied reasoning with VLA policy learning, achieving state-of-the-art results on WidowX and Google Robot benchmarks.
- AutoBio provides a simulation framework and benchmark designed to evaluate robotic automation in biology laboratory environments.
